[Bio]


I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University. I work in the areas of computer networks and distributed systems. Before joining Hopkins, I spent a year in the AMPLab/RISELab at UC Berkeley, working with Professor Ion Stoica. I received my PhD in the Department of Computer Science at Princeton University in June 2016, advised by Professor Jennifer Rexford. I am a Princeton Honorific Fellow and a Siebel Scholar.

I am looking for self-motivated students to work with me at Hopkins. Please apply here and mention me in your application materials. Also feel free to drop me an email with your CV.

Research

I work in computer networks and distributed systems, with a focus on programmable switches, software-defined networking, cloud computing, and big data systems.

  • Scaling distributed systems with programmable switches
    • In-network caching for distributed storage [NetCache, SOSP'17]
  • Low-latency data analytics systems
    • Network traffic analytics with sketches [SketchVisor, SIGCOMM'17]
  • Software-defined networking
    • Managing big data transfers on optical WANs [Owan, SIGCOMM'16]
    • Controller composition [CoVisor, NSDI'15]
    • Fast network update [Dionysus, SIGCOMM'14]
